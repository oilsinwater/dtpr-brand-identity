Visual Morphological Box

The morphological box (originated by astrophysicist Fritz Zwicky and introduced to design practice by Karl Gerstner) maps all system parameters along one axis and their corresponding options along the other. This framework enables designers to systematically review design choices while keeping the overall identity constraints in focus. Below, tracing the visual variables of the DTPR design sprint: select one pathway from Posture A to Posture D in each row to establish a unified visual grammar.

How to conduct this sprint:

  1. Select one option per row: Lock a combination of options (A to D) down to create your sprint recipe (e.g., "B-C-B-B-C-B").
  2. Sketch Assets: Use the chosen geometry, color rules, and typographic pairing to construct the 5 core adaptive assets (Symbol, Line, Frame, Label, Letters).
  3. Test applications: Place the assets on the three target layout formats (square sign, mobile scan layout, and wide desktop page) to verify responsiveness.
  4. Strategic Grounding: The options columns are not arbitrary; Columns A to D map directly to our target personas and strategic brand postures (Citizen Utility, Developer Blueprint, Advocate Commons, Deployer Compliance). See the Strategic Postures reference page for a detailed analysis of their definitions.
