What survived, what's fragile, what the Phase 3 sprint has to address

Six years of design work, four major surfaces, one Assembly Day convening, two competing visual systems, ten unresolved open questions. The brand identity audit is the inventory. The Phase 3 brand sprint is the work.

The five load-bearing constants

These exist on every iteration. The brand book has to treat them as load-bearing.

1 · The hexagon as the atomic unit

Every site uses the hex as container, frame, or mark. The hex is the standard's visual language, not just the brand mark.

The same hex value appears in all four light-mode sites. It is the brand. Everything else is negotiable.

Open-source is a non-negotiable visual signal. Appears in the footer of every page across every surface.

The six fragile areas

These are the places where the brand identity is in tension, contradictory, or under-specified. The Phase 3 brand sprint has to address each.

1 · Three type systems, no codification

dtpr.io (Red Hat Text), Makeshift (JetBrains Mono + Source Sans), vision.dtpr.io (system stack), docs.dtpr.io (Mintlify default). The brand has to commit to one stack — or to name the typographic grammar that lets each context pick.

Corporate Memphis (dtpr.io + Assembly Day) vs. hex-based scenarios (vision.dtpr.io). The brand book has to pick — or to name which goes where.

The three new findings from this audit

Things the foundation memo didn't surface that this audit caught.

1 · The Assembly Day deck contains a brand layer the web does not show

The cobalt #2D2BFF "Communication" callout cards, the amber #FFC107 "Capacity Building" cards, and the teal vertical rules in the journey-map layouts are a complete brand layer that exists only in the internal deck. The brand book should decide whether to adopt this layer on the web or to retire it.

page 10 · Assembly Day deck p14

2 · The Assembly Day Corporate Memphis is the canonical version, not the dtpr.io hero

The Community Conversation Kit cover (Assembly Day p25) is the most refined Corporate Memphis cityscape in the system — more complete than the dtpr.io homepage hero, with characters in transit, recycling station, storefronts, lampposts, multiple bicycles. If the team keeps Memphis, this is the version to keep, not the live dtpr.io hero.

Assembly Day deck p25

3 · docs.dtpr.io is in-family with the other three light-mode sites

(Self-audit correction, v0.2.1.) An earlier version incorrectly described docs.dtpr.io as a "dark mode" outlier. The actual site is light mode with a teal link accent (#0f5153). All four surfaces are light-mode, all four use the same teal/navy palette, all four use the same hex mark.

page 05

4 · The Message Map is a genuine brand voice fork, not a copy choice

The Message Map (June 2026) names two co-existing voice tracks: Track A (Trust as governance infrastructure) and Track B (Accountable Tech by Design). This is not a tone modulation — the two tracks require different register, different audience framing, and different tagline hierarchies. The brand book has to name and manage this fork explicitly, or the brand will continue to use them inconsistently across surfaces.

Adjacent open items (not pure brand, but constrain it)

  • Stack decision (Next.js vs. Nuxt continuity). Affects the technical surface but not the brand surface directly. Phase 2 in flight.
  • Feedback Hub. The brand promise of "find out → feed back" cannot close without it. — see consolidation plan Q1.6
  • Cross-navigation between the four surfaces. No header link back to dtpr.io from any of the other three. Heuristics synthesis H1 finding.
  • "DTPR for AI" story. MEMO No articulated narrative for AI deployments in the current ecosystem. The Assembly Day deck documents the AI taxonomy (73 elements) but there is no public-facing explanation of what DTPR means for AI systems vs. physical sensors. Gap identified in memo §9.
  • Engagement Journey Map. MEMO The Assembly Day workshop (May 2026) ran an Engagement Journey Map exercise. Outputs have not been integrated into any live surface or the brand system. The map would directly inform the brand promise's "find out → feed back" arc.
