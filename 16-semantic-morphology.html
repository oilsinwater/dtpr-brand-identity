Sprint Context & Rationale:

  1. Why a third box? The first box mapped Visual Form (Aesthetics). The second mapped Visual Process (Interactions). This third box maps Semantics, Policy & Narrative (Meaning)—the structural choices around deployment tracks, registers of transparency, feedback loops, organization framing, and education.
  2. Completing the Sprint Recipe: A complete DTPR design sprint recipe is formed by selecting exactly one cell from each of the three tables: Form recipe (p. 14) + Process recipe (p. 15) + Semantic recipe (p. 16). Together they locked down the entire design specification.
  3. Solving Gaps: Option C (Human/Ecosystem) focus variables are grounded in the Vigilant Advocate persona requirements, while Option D (Regulated/Standard) variables map directly to the compliance needs of public sector deployers.
  4. Strategic Grounding: The options columns are not arbitrary; Columns A to D map directly to our target personas and strategic brand postures (Citizen Utility, Developer Blueprint, Advocate Commons, Deployer Compliance). See the Strategic Postures reference page for a detailed analysis of their definitions.
