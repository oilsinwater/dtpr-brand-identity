The ISO 3864 collision — unresolved

Problem. The heuristics synthesis (dtpr-respectful-heuristics-synthesis-v2.md §6) flags that the visual ecology of DTPR's sign system collides with ISO 3864 public-safety color grammar:

  • DTPR's identifiability signal is the teal #0f5153 — currently paired with pale-teal #bae6ed in the system. In ISO 3864, teal/blue reads as "mandatory action" (think safety helmets, "you must wear this").
  • The Bootstrap-extracted yellow #ffc107 exists in the system (and appears in the Assembly Day cobalt + amber palette) but is not the identifiability color. If a future taxonomy uses it, it will collide with ISO's "warning / hazard" semantics.
  • There are no written co-placement rules for DTPR signs alongside ISO 7010 safety signs. The heuristic audit rates this as High risk for Pragmatic Deployers.

Options being considered in the heuristics synthesis:

  1. Remap the identifiability signal to a non-ISO hue.
  2. Mandate a DTPR-branded shape surround that visually differentiates DTPR marks from ISO safety shapes.
  3. Establish a two-tier icon treatment (high-picturability as standalone, low-picturability as QR-extended).

The brand book cannot lock color tokens without a decision here. OPEN

PROPOSED MEMO Chartreuse as candidate resolution: The brand foundation memo v2 (§5) notes that the MakeShift chartreuse (#c6e93d) sits outside ISO 3864 reserved hue families (red/orange/yellow/green). It could serve as the non-colliding accent while teal is remapped or given a mandatory shape surround. This is not a decision — it is a viable path worth evaluating.
