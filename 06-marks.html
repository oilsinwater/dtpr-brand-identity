06 · Marks & seals

The DTPR mark, in all its forms

The hexagon is the brand's atomic unit. The canonical mark is a hexagon with an inner circle and a small offset square. Three SVG variants (blue, black, outline) live on vision.dtpr.io. Plus three 2020-era proposal seals, plus an event-specific layered mark for Makeshift. This is the visual element everyone agrees on — the rest of the brand is in tension around it.

Geometry breakdown

Each variant is a hexagon with an inner circle and a small offset square inside the lower-right of the inner circle. The geometry is identical across the three; only the fill/stroke differs.

DTPR_Logo_Blue.svg (28×32 viewBox, blue solid):
  Outer hexagon path:  M14,0 L28,8 L28,24 L14,32 L0,24 L0,8 Z
  Inner circle:         r=8, centered at (14, 16) — visible as a "hole" via even-odd fill rule
  Offset square:        7×8 unit, positioned at (10, 12) inside the lower-right of the circle

Stewardship signatures

DTPR is stewarded by Helpful Places. The stewardship signature appears in the footer of every page. It is not a co-brand — it is a trust signal.

helpful-places black monochrome

The Helpful Places mark on dtpr.io is the hexagonal grid "H E L P F U L P L A C E S" arrangement — a typographic mark, not a logo in the conventional sense. It appears in the dtpr.io footer block as the steward of the standard.

Co-brand rules are pending the Stewardship Model sign-off. OPEN — see foundation memo §6

What the brand book has to decide

Recommended
  • Use the navy solid as the default mark
  • Adopt a 0.4× mark-height clearspace rule
  • Use the outline variant only on dark or busy backgrounds
  • Treat the layered hex as event-specific (not for general use)
Avoid
  • Recolor the mark in arbitrary hues
  • Add drop shadows, bevels, or 3D effects
  • Containerize the mark in shapes that fight its geometry
  • Use the event-specific MakeShift mark on a non-event surface

OPEN: canonical mark + wordmark lockup SVG. No formal lockup file exists. Each site constructs it by hand. The brand book should specify.

OPEN: clearspace and minimum size rules. No written rules exist. The 0.4× mark-height clearspace and 24px digital / 8mm print minimums are PROPOSED in the previous version of this audit; they need team sign-off.
